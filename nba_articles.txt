The 2023-24 NBA season was a historic one for the Boston Celtics. They finished the regular season with a league-best 64-18 record. In the playoffs, they demonstrated their dominance, culminating in a 4-1 series victory over the Dallas Mavericks in the NBA Finals. This victory marked the franchise's 18th championship, the most in NBA history.

Jaylen Brown was awarded the Bill Russell NBA Finals Most Valuable Player Award for his outstanding performance on both ends of the court. He averaged 20.8 points, 5.4 rebounds, and 5.0 assists in the series.

On the individual awards front, Nikola Jokic of the Denver Nuggets secured his third NBA Most Valuable Player (MVP) award, further cementing his legacy as one of the greatest centers of all time.

The Rookie of the Year race was dominated by Victor Wembanyama of the San Antonio Spurs. The French phenom had one of the most impressive rookie seasons in recent memory, also winning the Defensive Player of the Year award, a rare feat for a rookie.

The inaugural NBA In-Season Tournament took place in December 2023, with the Los Angeles Lakers emerging as the first-ever champions after defeating the Indiana Pacers in the final. LeBron James was named the tournament's MVP.
